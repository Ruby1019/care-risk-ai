# Research Background

## Research Topic

CareRisk AI focuses on AI-assisted healthcare process risk analysis and decision support.

The project aims to explore how healthcare process event logs can be transformed into actionable indicators for workflow monitoring, risk identification, and process improvement.

## Background

Healthcare services often involve complex workflows across multiple departments, such as registration, triage, consultation, medication, and discharge. During these processes, patients may experience long waiting times, repeated activities, missing steps, or abnormal process patterns.

These workflow issues may affect service quality, operational efficiency, patient experience, and healthcare resource allocation.

From the perspective of healthcare information systems, it is important to develop data-driven tools that can help managers and practitioners understand process performance and identify potential risks.

## Research Problem

Traditional healthcare process management often relies on manual review, administrative reports, or retrospective analysis. These approaches may be time-consuming and may not easily reveal case-level risks or workflow bottlenecks.

This project addresses the following research problem:

How can healthcare process event logs be used to identify workflow bottlenecks, calculate case-level risk scores, and support AI-assisted decision-making for healthcare process improvement?

## Research Objectives

The objectives of this project are:

1. To generate and analyze healthcare process event logs.
2. To calculate activity duration and waiting time between healthcare activities.
3. To identify delayed or abnormal patient cases.
4. To develop a rule-based case-level risk scoring method.
5. To export structured analysis results for dashboard visualization.
6. To explore future integration with AI-assisted improvement suggestions.

## Method Overview

The current prototype follows these steps:

1. Generate synthetic healthcare process event logs.
2. Calculate activity duration and waiting time.
3. Aggregate case-level process indicators.
4. Apply rule-based risk scoring.
5. Classify cases into Low, Medium, and High risk levels.
6. Export analysis results as JSON for future dashboard development.

## Expected Contribution

This project may contribute to healthcare information systems research by demonstrating how process event data can be transformed into risk indicators and decision-support outputs.

The project also provides a technical prototype that connects healthcare process management, data analytics, and AI-assisted decision support.

## Future Research Direction

Future work may extend this project by:

- Applying process mining techniques
- Adding explainable AI methods
- Comparing different risk scoring models
- Developing an interactive dashboard
- Evaluating the system with healthcare professionals
- Extending the prototype to real or anonymized healthcare datasets
